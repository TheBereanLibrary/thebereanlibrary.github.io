Creeds & Councils: A Doctrinal Timeline

Creeds and councils assist the church in confessing Scripture’s teaching; they remain subordinate to the Bible.

Contents 1. Early Creeds (2nd–5th c.) 2. Medieval & Reformation 3. Modern Evangelical Statements 4. Key Lines from the Creeds Annotated Bibliography Notes

1. Early Creeds (2nd–5th c.)

  • Apostles’ Creed (2nd–8th c.).
  • Nicaea (325): Son consubstantial with the Father (John 1:1; 10:30).
  • Constantinople (381): Full deity of the Spirit (Acts 5:3–4).
  • Chalcedon (451): One person, two natures (John 1:14; Phil 2:5–11).

2. Medieval & Reformation

  • Third Constantinople (680–681): Two wills in Christ.
  • Augsburg Confession (1530); Thirty-Nine Articles (1563); Westminster Confession (1646).

3. Modern Evangelical Statements

  • Lausanne Covenant (1974): Authority of Scripture; world evangelization (Matt 28:18–20).
  • Manila Manifesto (1989): Evangelism and social responsibility.

4. Key Lines from the Creeds

“Very God of very God… of one substance with the Father.” — Nicene Creed (short excerpt)
“One and the same Christ, Son, Lord, Only-begotten, acknowledged in two natures.” — Chalcedon (short excerpt)

  • Philip Schaff, Creeds of Christendom. Texts and historical notes.
