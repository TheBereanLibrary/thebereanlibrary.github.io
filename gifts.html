Spiritual Gifts: Biblical Foundations, Perspectives, and Practice

The Spirit grants diverse gifts for the common good and the mission of the church (1 Cor 12:7; Eph 4:11–16).

Contents 1. Foundations and Purpose 2. Gift Lists 3. Cessationist Emphases 4. Continuationist Emphases 5. Wise Pastoral Practice 6. Historical Notes Annotated Bibliography Notes

1. Foundations and Purpose

Gifts are sovereignly distributed (1 Cor 12:11) with love as the greatest (1 Cor 13). The body metaphor insists on mutual dependence (1 Cor 12:12–27).

2. Gift Lists

Romans 12:6–8; 1 Corinthians 12:8–10,28–30; Ephesians 4:11; 1 Peter 4:10–11 are overlapping, non-exhaustive. Edification and intelligibility regulate their use (1 Cor 14:12,26–33,40).

3. Cessationist Emphases

Revelatory/sign gifts belonged to the church’s foundation (Eph 2:20; Heb 2:3–4). Emphasize Scripture’s sufficiency and ordinary means.

4. Continuationist Emphases

Gifts may continue; must be tested (1 Thess 5:19–21; 1 John 4:1) and ordered (1 Cor 14:29–33). Aim: love and mission (Acts 2:17–18).

5. Wise Pastoral Practice

  • Prioritize love, order, and edification.
  • Encourage broad service; avoid elitism; honor weaker members (1 Cor 12:22–26).
  • Keep Scripture as final authority (Acts 17:11).

6. Historical Notes

Early church reports of prophecy/discernment (Didache; Justin Martyr). Patristic testimony varies; the mainstream locates authority in apostolic Scripture.

Annotated Bibliography

  • D. A. Carson, Showing the Spirit. Exposition of 1 Corinthians 12–14.
  • Sam Storms, Practicing the Power. Continuationist perspective with pastoral cautions.
  • Richard B. Gaffin Jr., Perspectives on Pentecost. Redemptive-historical/cessationist emphasis.
