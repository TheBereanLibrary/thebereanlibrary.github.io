About The Berean Library

“These were more noble… for they received the word with all eagerness, examining the Scriptures daily to see if these things were so.” — Acts 17:11

Mission

To serve the global church and curious seekers with resources that are biblically faithful (2 Tim 3:16–17; Ps 19:7–11), Christ-centered (Luke 24:27; John 5:39), and edifying (Eph 4:11–16), encouraging Berean-like discernment (Acts 17:11) and mature discipleship (Col 1:28–29).

Vision

We envision a trusted, free library where the best of biblical scholarship meets accessible discipleship—bridging the classroom and the local church (Neh 8:1–8; Acts 20:20,27). Our north star is truth in love (Eph 4:15), worship in spirit and truth (John 4:24), and a people equipped for every good work (2 Tim 3:17).

Why “Berean”?

The Bereans were commended not for credulity but for careful, daily testing of teaching by Scripture (Acts 17:11). We aim to cultivate that noble posture across traditions while remaining anchored in the essentials confessed by the historic, orthodox church (cf. 1 Cor 15:3–4; Jude 3).

Core Commitments

  • Scripture — God-breathed, sufficient, clear, and authoritative (Deut 29:29; Isa 40:8; Matt 5:17–18; 2 Pet 1:19–21).
  • Orthodoxy — In continuity with the Apostles’ and Nicene Creeds and the Chalcedonian Definition, while avoiding sectarianism (2 Thess 2:15).
  • Scholarship — Rigorous, charitable engagement with primary sources and the whole counsel of God (Acts 20:27; Prov 18:17).
  • Edification — Resources crafted to build up believers and invite seekers (Rom 15:2; 1 Pet 3:15–16).
  • Integrity — Transparent sourcing, versioning, and correction when needed (Prov 12:17; Jas 3:1).

What We Offer

  • In‑Depth Q&A spanning biblical studies, theology, ethics, history, and Christian practice (Acts 8:30–35).
  • Primary Text Windows — guided connections to Scripture, early creeds, councils, and classic teachers.
  • Study Helps — word studies, outlines, and reading plans (Ps 1; Josh 1:8).
  • Christian Living — resources on prayer, vocation, family, mission (Matt 6:9–13; Col 3:12–17; Matt 28:18–20).
“Test everything; hold fast what is good.” — 1 Thessalonians 5:21

Historical anchoring: Apostles’ Creed; Nicene Creed (325, 381); Chalcedon (451). We cite these as concise summaries of biblical doctrine, not as authorities over Scripture.
