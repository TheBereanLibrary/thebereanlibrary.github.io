About The Berean Library

“Now these Jews were more noble than those in Thessalonica; they received the word with all eagerness, examining the Scriptures daily to see if these things were so.” (Acts 17:11)

The Berean Library exists to serve the global church and the curious public with a carefully curated, theologically rigorous knowledge base of Christianity. Our work seeks to be catholic (universal in scope), evangelical (centered on the gospel), and reformational (always being reformed by the Word of God).

Mission

To cultivate a trustworthy, accessible, and academically serious library that equips readers to test all things by Scripture, love the truth, and walk in wisdom (Prov 1:7; John 17:17; Eph 4:11–16).

Vision

We envision a generation of “Bereans” who examine the Scriptures daily, informed by the best of historical and contemporary scholarship, united around the core of the faith, and generous on secondary matters (Acts 17:11; Rom 14; 1 Cor 15:1–4).

Convictions

  • Scripture first. Scripture is our norming norm; tradition is honored as a guide under the Word (2 Tim 3:16–17; 2 Pet 1:19–21).
  • Historic orthodoxy. We confess the Trinitarian and Christological heart of the ecumenical creeds (Nicene, Chalcedonian, Apostles’).
  • Charitable breadth. We encourage generous engagement where faithful Christians disagree (Rom 14; Eph 4:1–6).
  • Scholarly excellence. We pursue accuracy, citation, and transparency, drawing on reputable academic sources.

What We Publish

  • Peer-reviewed style Q&A and long-form essays with Scripture proofs and historical notes.
  • Guides to biblical books, theology, church history, and Christian ethics.
  • Annotated bibliographies for deeper study.

Editorial Standards

All substantive articles undergo a multi-step editorial process: primary exegesis, theological comparison across traditions, historical-theological review, and fact-checking of citations.

Statement of Faith

We affirm the essentials of the Christian faith summarized in our Doctrinal Statement.
