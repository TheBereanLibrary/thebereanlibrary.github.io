Organizational Structure & Governance

“Let all things be done decently and in order.” — 1 Corinthians 14:40

1) Biblical Foundations

  • Qualifications: Leaders must meet 1 Tim 3:1–13; Titus 1:5–9; 1 Pet 5:1–4.
  • Wisdom in Counsel: “In an abundance of counselors there is safety” (Prov 11:14; 15:22).
  • Accountability & Correction: Matt 18:15–17; Gal 6:1; James 3:1.
  • Doctrinal Fidelity: 2 Tim 1:13–14; 2:2; Titus 2:1; Jude 3.

2) Organizational Bodies

A. Theological Council

Role: Guard doctrinal integrity; approve statements and sensitive resources; adjudicate disputes on theological matters (Acts 15:6–29).

Composition: 5–9 vetted individuals affirming the Doctrinal Statement, skilled in biblical studies, theology, or church history.

Process: Annual reaffirmation of beliefs; conflict‑of‑interest disclosures; minutes retained for transparency (Prov 10:9).

B. Executive Director

Leads strategy, partnerships, and operations; implements Council decisions; ensures compliance with policies (1 Cor 14:40).

C. Editorial Committee

Peer‑reviews all content for accuracy, tone, and clarity; consults at least two Council members for doctrinally sensitive topics (Acts 18:26; 2 Tim 2:15).

D. Advisory Panel

Subject‑matter experts (languages, exegesis, ethics, history, pastoral ministry) who provide non‑binding guidance. All advisors affirm the Doctrinal Statement.

3) Content Lifecycle & Review

  1. Submission → author affirms originality, sources, and doctrinal alignment.
  2. Editorial Review → style, clarity, citations.
  3. Doctrinal Review → two reviewers + Council sign‑off for sensitive topics (Gal 1:8–9).
  4. Publication → versioned release; public change log for substantive edits.
  5. Correction → transparent errata policy (Prov 12:1; 28:13).

4) Ethics & Conduct

  • Citation Integrity — honest attribution to Scripture and sources (Exod 20:16).
  • Pastoral Tone — truth in love; avoid needless polemics (Eph 4:15; 2 Tim 2:24–25).
  • Confidentiality & Privacy — protect contributors and correspondents (Prov 11:13).
  • Financial Transparency — if donations are received, maintain clear reporting (2 Cor 8:20–21).

5) Dispute Resolution

Follow Matthew 18:15–17 privately where possible; escalate to Council when necessary; document decisions; allow measured public statements when edifying and required (Rom 12:18).

6) Succession & Continuity

Annual review of key roles; written handover procedures; archival backups of all content; legal documents maintained and updated; doctrinal interviews for replacements (2 Tim 2:2).

7) Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, remaining assets transfer to a like‑minded, confessionally aligned ministry affirming this Doctrinal Statement in full.
