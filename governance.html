Governance

Governance & Editorial Policy

We pursue institutional integrity shaped by Scripture, historic wisdom, and best practices for theological publishing.

Contents 1. Biblical & Institutional Principles 2. Organizational Structure 3. Editorial Policy & Peer Review 4. Ethics, Conflicts, & Corrections 5. Intellectual Property & Licensing 6. Security & Data Protection

1. Biblical & Institutional Principles

  • Accountability. We seek plurality and transparency (Prov 11:14; Acts 14:23; 1 Pet 5:1–4).
  • Truthfulness. We commit to accuracy and integrity (Exod 20:16; Eph 4:25).
  • Edification. Content aims for the building up of the church (Eph 4:11–16).

2. Organizational Structure

  • Advisory Council. Seasoned pastors/academics provide theological oversight and long-term guidance.
  • Editorial Board. Subject editors (OT, NT, Systematic, Historical, Ethics) coordinate peer review and style.
  • Managing Editor. Ensures process discipline, schedules, and quality control.

3. Editorial Policy & Peer Review

  1. Submission. Articles and Q&A proposals include thesis, Scripture proofs, and bibliography.
  2. Initial Screening. Managing Editor checks scope, originality, and doctrinal alignment.
  3. Double Review. Two qualified reviewers assess exegesis, theology, and historical accuracy.
  4. Revision & Acceptance. Authors respond to reviewer notes; final acceptance by Editorial Board.
  5. Publication. Copyedit, typeset, and publish with DOIs/perm-links for stability.

4. Ethics, Conflicts, & Corrections

  • Conflict of Interest. Contributors disclose institutional and financial ties.
  • Corrections & Retractions. Documented errata with time-stamped changelogs; serious errors may prompt retraction.
  • Attribution. Quotations and ideas are credited; plagiarism is prohibited.

5. Intellectual Property & Licensing

Original content © The Berean Library; released for non-commercial educational use with attribution (CC BY-NC 4.0 recommended). Scripture quotations follow the terms of the respective translation. We avoid reproducing third-party content beyond fair use; images are either original, licensed, or public domain.

6. Security & Data Protection

  • Site Security. HTTPS enforced; dependencies monitored; backups maintained.
  • Privacy. Minimal data collection; no sale of personal data.
