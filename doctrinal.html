Beliefs

Doctrinal Statement

“The faith that was once for all delivered to the saints.” — Jude 3

Preamble

We confess the triune God and the gospel of Jesus Christ as revealed in the Holy Scriptures. We embrace the theological core expressed in the Apostles’ and Nicene Creeds and the Chalcedonian Definition, while affirming the Reformation solas. We receive the church’s doctrinal heritage as a help, yet submit all teaching to Scripture (Acts 17:11; 2 Tim 3:16–17; Isa 8:20).

1. Scripture (Bibliology)

The sixty‑six canonical books are God‑breathed (2 Tim 3:16–17), produced by men carried along by the Spirit (2 Pet 1:20–21), true and enduring (Ps 12:6; 19:7–11; 119:89; Matt 5:17–18; John 17:17). Scripture is sufficient for life and godliness (2 Pet 1:3), clear in its saving message (Deut 30:11–14; Ps 119:130), and the final authority for faith and practice (Acts 20:27; Gal 1:8–9).

2. God (Theology Proper)

There is one God (Deut 6:4; Isa 45:5–6), eternally existing in three Persons—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—of one essence and glory (Matt 28:19; 2 Cor 13:14; John 1:1; Acts 5:3–4). God is self‑existent (Exod 3:14), immutable (Mal 3:6), omniscient (Ps 139:1–6), omnipresent (Ps 139:7–12), omnipotent (Jer 32:17), holy (Isa 6:3), just and merciful (Exod 34:6–7), and sovereign over all (Ps 115:3; Dan 4:35; Eph 1:11).

3. Christ (Christology)

Jesus Christ is the eternal Son, true God and true man (John 1:1,14; Col 2:9). Conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary (Luke 1:35; Matt 1:23), He lived sinlessly (Heb 4:15; 1 Pet 2:22), taught with divine authority (Matt 7:28–29), worked signs (John 20:30–31), died a substitutionary, atoning death (Isa 53:4–6; Mark 10:45; Rom 3:24–26; 2 Cor 5:21; 1 Pet 3:18), rose bodily (Luke 24:36–43; 1 Cor 15:3–8), ascended (Acts 1:9–11), and will return in glory (Matt 24:30; Rev 22:20). We affirm the hypostatic union and the communicatio idiomatum as articulated at Chalcedon (451).

4. The Holy Spirit (Pneumatology)

The Holy Spirit is the divine Person who proceeds from the Father and the Son (John 14:16–17,26; 15:26), convicting the world (John 16:8–11), regenerating (John 3:5–8; Titus 3:5), indwelling (Rom 8:9–11; 1 Cor 6:19), sealing (Eph 1:13–14), gifting (1 Cor 12:4–11), sanctifying (2 Thess 2:13; Gal 5:16–25), and empowering for witness (Acts 1:8). He glorifies Christ (John 16:14) and illuminates Scripture (1 Cor 2:10–14).

5. Humanity & Sin (Anthropology & Hamartiology)

Humans are created male and female in God’s image (Gen 1:26–28; 2:24), with dignity and vocation (Ps 8). In Adam all sinned and died (Gen 3; Rom 5:12–19; 3:9–23; Eph 2:1–3). Sin is lawlessness (1 John 3:4) that incurs guilt and corruption, rendering humanity unable to save itself (Jer 17:9; John 6:44).

6. Salvation (Soteriology)

Salvation is by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone (Eph 2:8–9; Rom 3:21–26; 5:1). Key benefits include effectual calling (Rom 8:30), regeneration (John 3:5–8), conversion—repentance and faith (Acts 20:21; Mark 1:15), justification (Rom 3:24; 4:5; 5:1), adoption (John 1:12; Gal 4:4–7), sanctification (1 Thess 4:3; Heb 12:14), perseverance (John 10:27–29; Phil 1:6), and glorification (Rom 8:30). We affirm union with Christ as the fountain of every spiritual blessing (Eph 1:3; Rom 6:1–11; Col 3:1–4).

7. The Church (Ecclesiology)

Christ builds His church (Matt 16:18); the universal church is His body (Eph 1:22–23; 4:4–6; 1 Cor 12). Local churches gather for Word, prayer, fellowship, and the ordinances (Acts 2:42; 1 Tim 4:13). Baptism and the Lord’s Supper are appointed by Christ (Matt 28:19–20; Luke 22:19–20; 1 Cor 11:23–26) as signs and proclamations of the gospel; we welcome conscientious differences of practice within historic orthodoxy. Qualified leaders (1 Tim 3:1–13; Titus 1:5–9; 1 Pet 5:1–4) shepherd the flock. The church’s mission is to make disciples of all nations (Matt 28:18–20; Acts 1:8).

8. Christian Life (Ethics & Discipleship)

God’s people are called to holiness and love (Lev 19:2; 1 Pet 1:14–16; John 13:34–35), empowered by the Spirit (Rom 8:13–14), walking by faith (2 Cor 5:7) and bearing the fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22–23). We uphold the sanctity of life (Gen 1:27; Ps 139:13–16), marriage as a covenantal union between one man and one woman (Gen 2:24; Matt 19:4–6; Eph 5:31–33), justice and mercy (Mic 6:8), integrity (Prov 12:22), generosity (2 Cor 9:6–8), and peacemaking (Matt 5:9).

9. Last Things (Eschatology)

We await the personal, visible return of Christ (Acts 1:11; 1 Thess 4:13–18), the resurrection of the dead (John 5:28–29; 1 Cor 15), final judgment (Acts 17:31; Rev 20:11–15), and the new heavens and new earth (Isa 65:17; 66:22; 2 Pet 3:13; Rev 21–22). Believers will enjoy everlasting communion with God (Rev 21:3–4), while the impenitent face everlasting separation (Matt 25:31–46).

Interpretive Method

We employ the grammatical‑historical method, reading Scripture in its canonical context, with Christ as the telos of God’s redemptive plan (Luke 24:25–27; 1 Pet 1:10–12). The Spirit illumines the text to the church (1 Cor 2:10–16) and calls us to obey what we understand (Jas 1:22).

Historical touchstones: Apostles’ Creed; Nicene Creed (325/381); Chalcedonian Definition (451); Reformation solas. These summaries are valued insofar as they faithfully echo Scripture.
