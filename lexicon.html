Mini Lexicon (Greek & Hebrew)

Original‑language anchors to aid exegesis (not to replace context). Transliteration and core semantic ranges included.

χάρις (charis) — Grace

Favor, gift; in Paul, salvific grace that empowers (Eph 2:8–9; Titus 2:11).

πίστις (pistis) — Faith

Trust, faithfulness; context determines emphasis (Rom 3:22, 28; Heb 11).

δικαιοσύνη (dikaiosynē) — Righteousness

God’s righteous character and the status He grants in Christ (Rom 1:17; 3:21–26).

ἀγάπη (agapē) — Love

Self‑giving love rooted in God’s nature (John 3:16; 1 John 4:7–10).

תּוֹרָה (torah) — Instruction/Law

Instruction of God; covenantal guidance (Ps 1; Deut 6; Rom 7).

חֶסֶד (ḥesed) — Steadfast Love

Covenant loyalty/mercy (Exod 34:6–7; Ps 136).

בְּרִית (berit) — Covenant

Binding arrangement grounded in divine promise (Gen 15; Jer 31:31–34).

πνεῦμα (pneuma) — Spirit

Holy Spirit, breath, spirit; personal divine agent (Acts 5:3–4; Rom 8).
